# Example 2: Congestion Pricing Decision

## Decision

A city is considering congestion pricing to reduce traffic, emissions, and transit delay.

Options:

- **A. No change**
- **B. Flat congestion charge**
- **C. Congestion charge with low-income exemptions, transit reinvestment, and accessibility review**

## Cascade lesson

A flat charge may score well on emissions and efficiency while burdening low-income commuters. RippleLogic forces the city to check rights and floor conditions before treating aggregate benefits as sufficient.

## RF/NCRC

Check access to work, disability travel needs, due process for fines, appeal mechanisms, and discriminatory burden.

## TRC

Check systemic risk: emergency access, displacement of traffic into vulnerable neighborhoods, political legitimacy collapse, and transit overload.

## CSV

Check execution viability: cameras, billing, appeals, exemptions, enforcement, privacy, reinvestment governance, and monitoring.

## RLS

After redesign, score remaining options across households, communities, polity legitimacy, city organizations, humanity/climate, and biosphere effects. RLS should make visible the trade between emissions benefits, equity protections, trust, administrative burden, and health impacts.
