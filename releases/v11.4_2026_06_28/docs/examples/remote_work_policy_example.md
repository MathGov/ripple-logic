# Example 1: Remote Work Policy Decision

**Purpose:** A lightweight Tier 2 example showing how the cascade changes the question from "Which policy is popular?" to "Which policy is grounded, rights-safe, tail-safe, structurally viable, and then welfare-positive?"

## Decision

A mid-size organization is deciding between three work policies:

- **A. Full office return**
- **B. Hybrid default**
- **C. Employee-choice remote-first**

## RG - Reality Grounding

Evidence needed: job functions, productivity data, employee-care obligations, office constraints, team coordination needs, disability and caregiving impacts, legal obligations, cybersecurity risks, and attrition patterns.

Claim boundary: without longitudinal data, the run may compare plausible risks and benefits but must not claim one policy is empirically proven superior.

## RF - Rights Floor

Check whether any option creates non-compensatory harms: disability exclusion, retaliation for protected care responsibilities, privacy invasion through excessive monitoring, unequal access to advancement, or unsafe workplace conditions.

If full office return would predictably exclude protected workers without accommodation, it fails or requires redesign.

## TRC - Tail-Risk Constraint

Tail risks include mass attrition in critical teams, severe cybersecurity exposure, legal/regulatory failure, and organizational breakdown. These are usually not existential, but may still require Tier 2 scenario review.

## CSV - Containment and Structural Viability

Check whether the policy can actually be administered fairly. A remote-first policy without onboarding, cybersecurity, escalation, and manager training may look welfare-positive but fail structural viability.

CSV asks whether local flexibility hollows organizational coherence, or whether office return hollows employee trust and retention.

## RLS - Residual welfare scoring

Only redesigned/selectable policies are ranked. Relevant welfare dimensions include material, health, social, knowledge, agency, meaning, and environment across affected scopes.

A likely result is not a universal answer, but a scoped recommendation such as: "Hybrid default with accommodation rights, transparent monitoring limits, quarterly review, manager training, and role-specific exceptions."
