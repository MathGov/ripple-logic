# Release Claims and Non-Claims

## Claims

This package is a Tier 1-3 public documentation/source release for the MathGov framework using the RippleLogic decision architecture. It contains the governing Canon, SGP companion, wrapper standard, agent-system companion, primer, worked-run exemplar, compact standards, and release-support files.

## Non-claims

This release is not empirical validation, not legal certification, not deployment certification, not ProofPack-ready, not Tier 4-ready, not a reference calculator, not a completed JSON-schema/validator package, and not automated moral truth.

RLS cannot rescue failed Reality Grounding, RF/NCRC, TRC, or CSV. SGP protection does not imply governance authority. All-Encompassing Infinite Union (AIU) is a horizon orientation, not a scoring object or proof.

## v11.4 hardening claim boundary

The new hardening guides are governance and validation scaffolding. They do not convert the release into empirical validation, legal certification, deployment certification, ProofPack, Tier 4, reference-calculator status, or automated moral truth.

## Computability and realizability boundary

Claimable: MathGov states that computed, simulated, generated, or model-fluent possibilities do not become decision-authoritative merely because they can be represented. They remain candidates until the cascade establishes grounded claim authority, Rights Floor/NCRC pass, TRC pass, CSV pass or controlled pass, and downstream RLS ranking where applicable.

Not claimable: this release does not implement a realizability-native computer architecture, physics engine, formal-methods kernel, or proof system that prevents unrealizable states from being represented. It is a realizability-aware governance and selectability architecture, not a replacement for future substrate-level work.


## v11.4 Final Core

This patch may claim final documentation-source polishing before validation work. It may not claim empirical validation, legal certification, deployment certification, ProofPack readiness, Tier 4 readiness, reference-calculator status, or automated moral truth.
