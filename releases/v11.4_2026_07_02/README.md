## What this is

MathGov is an open ethical-governance framework. RippleLogic is the decision architecture inside MathGov. This package specifies a decision cascade for high-consequence choices where ordinary single-score optimization can hide rights harms, tail risks, externalities, or structural failure.

Public cascade:

**RG -> RF -> TRC -> CSV -> RLS**

Plain language:

**Ground reality -> protect rights -> bound ruin -> preserve the structure -> score the ripples.**

Formal shorthand:

**RG/RSG -> RF/NCRC -> TRC -> CSV -> RLS**

RF is the public **Rights Floor** layer. NCRC is the formal **Non-Compensatory Rights Constraint** predicate that implements that layer. CSV means **Containment and Structural Viability**. RLS ranks only options that survive Reality Grounding claim support, RF/NCRC, TRC, and CSV.

## Computability vs realizability

This release makes explicit a boundary already implied by RG and CSV: **computable is not selectable**. A system may compute, simulate, or generate a possible state that is physically impossible, structurally unstable, rights-unsafe, ruin-exposed, or not executable under real constraints. RippleLogic therefore treats generated outputs as candidates only. They gain decision standing only after the cascade establishes reality contact, rights protection, tail-risk bounding, structural viability, and residual welfare ranking.

v11.4.6 confirms the current hierarchy across the release: MathGov is the umbrella framework, RippleLogic is the ethical decision operating system and decision architecture inside MathGov, and SGP is the companion moral-status and rights-of-protection protocol. It also preserves the refreshed Aligners Sheet calculation surface so workbook consumers see the worked-run values.

This release also adds **Source-Coupling Integrity** as a MathGov-native hardening layer inside Reality Grounding and CSV. A downstream output, model-fluent answer, benchmark result, inherited procedure, compliance state, or institutional permission is not proof of grounded capability. When material, the run must record the enabling conditions, boundary conditions, source evidence, inherited assumptions, downstream compensations, source-coupling status, and required claim action.
