# MathGov Essentials

This is the shortest faithful doorway into the release.

## One sentence

MathGov is an open ethical-governance framework; RippleLogic is its decision architecture for making high-consequence choices more reality-grounded, rights-safe, tail-safe, structurally viable, and auditable.

## The five-step cascade

1. **RG - Reality Grounding.** State what is known, unknown, evidenced, assumed, and claimable.
2. **RF - Rights Floor.** Protect non-compensatory rights and protected interests. Formally implemented by NCRC.
3. **TRC - Tail-Risk Constraint.** Bound catastrophic, irreversible, or ruinous downside. Do not average ruin away.
4. **CSV - Containment and Structural Viability.** Check whether harms are contained, routed, monitored, and whether the option can structurally stand.
5. **RLS - RippleLogic Score.** Rank remaining selectable options across the welfare field. RLS never rescues a failed gate.

## How a decision run works

1. Define the decision and options.
2. Declare the reality surface and claim boundary.
3. Identify stakeholders and mapped Union Scopes.
4. Check RF/NCRC.
5. Check TRC.
6. Check CSV.
7. Score residual welfare with RLS only for options that remain selectable.
8. Record the PCC/audit trail.
9. State what is selected, refused, narrowed, provisional, or escalated.

## What makes it different

RippleLogic separates **admissibility** from **ranking**. Ordinary optimization asks, "Which option scores highest?" RippleLogic asks first: "Which options are grounded, rights-safe, tail-safe, and structurally viable enough to be selectable?" Only then does it score residual welfare.

## Computability vs realizability

Modern AI can generate enormous possibility spaces. MathGov does not treat those possibilities as decision authority. **Computable is not selectable. Generated is not grounded. Executable is not ethical. Realizable is necessary but not sufficient.** RG, RF/NCRC, TRC, CSV, and RLS turn raw possibility into governed selectability only when the candidate survives the full cascade.

## Source-Coupling Integrity

MathGov also checks whether a claimed capability remains traceable to its enabling conditions and limits. A fluent model output, high benchmark score, inherited procedure, compliance label, or institutional permission is not by itself proof that the capability is grounded. When material, Source-Coupling Integrity records the claimed capability, enabling conditions, boundary conditions, source evidence, inherited assumptions, compensating controls, and required claim action. It is not a sixth gate; it strengthens RG and CSV.

## v11.4 hardening note

RippleLogic improves the structure of ethical decision-making; it does not make poor evidence, missing stakeholders, weak scenarios, captured institutions, or invalid thresholds reliable.

For stronger implementation, use the hardening guides in `docs/guides/` and validation scaffolding in `docs/validation/`:

- gate-boundary discrimination keeps TRC, CSV, and RLS distinct;
- rights-threshold governance protects RF/NCRC from arbitrary threshold movement;
- subgroup discovery reduces average-person erasure;
- Emergency Mode uses least-rights-infringing provisional action, not ordinary welfare compensation;
- TRC scenario discovery improves the search for ruin paths;
- CSV maturity declarations prevent overclaiming structural measurement;
- PCC profiles make audit burden proportional to stakes;
- HDW capture checks protect democratic tuning;
- SGP validation scaffolding tests protection-surface reliability;
- stewardship evasion audits prevent consequential influence from hiding behind informational labels;
- the Meta-Union Firewall keeps the All-Encompassing Infinite Union (AIU) as horizon orientation, not a Tier 1-3 scoring object;
- the Computability vs Realizability Bridge keeps generated or computable possibilities from being mistaken for grounded, selectable, executable, or ethical decisions.
