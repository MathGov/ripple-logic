# HDW Participation and Capture Guide

**Status:** companion governance guide. Canon controls if conflict occurs.

## Purpose

Hybrid Democratic Weighting combines constitutional floors, affected-stakeholder voice, and structural evidence. This guide reduces the risk that democratic tuning becomes captured, performative, exclusionary, or manipulable.

## Participation record

A Tier 3 HDW process SHOULD record:

| Field | Required content |
|---|---|
| affected_groups | groups or scopes materially affected |
| representation_method | direct input, representatives, consultation, expert proxy, deliberative process, or mixed method |
| selection_rationale | why this representation method was used |
| exclusion_risks | who may be missing or underrepresented |
| capture_risks | incentives, sponsors, authority pressure, strategic behavior, information asymmetry |
| minority_report | dissenting or non-majority concerns |
| correction_action | mitigation for missing or weak representation |

## Capture checks

Ask:

1. Who controls the agenda?
2. Who controls the evidence?
3. Who benefits from the weights?
4. Who cannot participate but is affected?
5. Who is represented only by others?
6. Are participants informed enough to make meaningful proposals?
7. Are there retaliation or conformity pressures?
8. Would an independent challenger accept the process as fair enough for the claim made?

## Optional democratic methods

Quadratic voting, deliberative polling, citizen juries, participatory budgeting, expert-stakeholder panels, and affected-party assemblies MAY be used when suitable. None are canonical defaults. Each requires context-specific governance.

## Anti-erasure rule

Democratic tuning cannot lower constitutional floors, erase non-vocal stakeholders, remove protected cells, or weaken RF/NCRC, TRC, or CSV.

## Plain-language rule

Voice improves the weights only when the people bearing the ripples can meaningfully affect the tuning, and when the floor cannot be voted away.
