# Example 3: AI Tutor Adoption in Education

## Decision

A university is deciding whether to deploy an AI tutor in a high-enrollment module.

Options:

- **A. Do not deploy**
- **B. Deploy general-purpose AI tutor**
- **C. Deploy bounded AI tutor with teacher oversight, privacy controls, appeal path, accessibility testing, and assessment-assurance limits**

## Key point

The question is not simply whether the AI tutor raises average learning outcomes. The cascade checks whether the system is grounded, rights-safe, tail-safe, structurally viable, and only then welfare-positive.

## RG

Evidence: learning outcome basis, student data exposure, hallucination rate, accessibility, dependency effects, teacher workload, equity impact, and assessment validity.

## RF

Protect privacy, non-discrimination, due process, accessibility, student agency, and academic fairness.

## TRC

Bound tail risks such as mass misinformation, high-stakes assessment contamination, severe privacy breach, or institutional dependency on ungoverned vendor systems.

## CSV

Check whether the university can actually govern the tool: oversight staffing, escalation routes, audit logs, procurement controls, data governance, student notice, and failure response.

## RLS

Only after those gates are passed should the remaining options be scored for learning, equity, workload, agency, trust, material cost, and institutional resilience.
