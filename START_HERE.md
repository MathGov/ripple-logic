## Core Cascade

The public RippleLogic method is:

**Reality Grounding → Rights Floor → TRC → CSV → RLS**

Formal shorthand:

**RG → RF → TRC → CSV → RLS**

Where:

- **Reality Grounding** establishes what is known, unknown, evidenced, uncertain, constrained, and claimable.
- **Rights Floor** protects non-compensatory rights before welfare or benefit scoring.
- **TRC**, the **Tail-Risk Constraint**, bounds catastrophic, irreversible, and ruin-scale risks.
- **CSV**, **Core Structure Viability**, checks whether the action preserves the basic structures needed for life, agency, trust, governance, and long-term flourishing.
- **RLS**, **RippleLogic Scoring**, ranks only options that survive the upstream levels.

Plain-language version:

1. Ground reality.
2. Protect rights.
3. Bound ruin.
4. Preserve the structure.
5. Score the ripples.

A high welfare or benefit score cannot compensate for a failed Rights Floor, failed Tail-Risk Constraint, or failed Core Structure Viability check.
